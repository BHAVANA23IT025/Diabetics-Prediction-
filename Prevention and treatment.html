Prevention and treatment

Prevention and treatment report extracted from WHO(World Health Organisation)

Prevention of Type 2 Diabetes and Complications:

Type 1 diabetes cannot currently be prevented.

Effective strategies exist to prevent Type 2 diabetes and its complications.

Key practices for good health include:

• Regular exercise
• Healthy eating
• Avoiding smoking
• Controlling blood pressure and lipids

Importance of Early Diagnosis:

• Early diagnosis is crucial for better health outcomes in diabetes.
• Easy access to basic diagnostics, like blood glucose testing, should be available in primary health care settings.
• .Periodic specialist assessments or treatment for complications are necessary.

Cost-Effective Interventions for Better Patient Outcomes:

• Blood glucose control through diet, physical activity, and medication if needed.
• Control of blood pressure and lipids to reduce cardiovascular risk and complications.
• Regular screening for eye, kidney, and foot damage to enable early treatment.
